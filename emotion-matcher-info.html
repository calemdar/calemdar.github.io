About Me

This game was made with a single goal in mind. To evoke an emotion in the player towards what they see on screen. The best way to make someone remember anything is to connect it with a corresponding feeling in their head. This is not as simple as it sounds, because the emotion has to be an intuitive reaction by the user. With this game, we tried giving the player a target emotion shown by another person in the video, and ask them to mimic the same emotion. Even though the computer vision API we used was not perfect in reading the emotions off of people's faces, we achieved our goal of evoking a certain emotion on all of our playtesters. They all laughed multiple times. There were different parts that made each person laugh, but we saw that asking someone to mimic a smile always lead the player to laugh or at least smirk afterwards.

We came up with the idea for an emotion matcher game from Guitar Hero. In Guitar hero the player has to hit the right note at the right time, and we wanted the user to hit the right emotion at the right time. However, since the API we were using was not great at reading the emotion accurately, we decided on pausing the video and waiting for the user to show the corresponding emotion. If you want to read more about the game and what went into the design you can check out the Medium article on it.
