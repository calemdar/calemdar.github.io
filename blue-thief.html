Blue Thief


This is a puzzle game that takes place in an art museum. The player can walk around and look at the paintings on the walls and read information about them. The player has to collect cues and find the code to open the door. But be careful of the security cameras! This was made with Game Maker and developped by me, Myles Karam, Peter Nolan, and Kit Campolieta.

I was in charge of the level design, shadowing, and interaction with the environment. I designed the levels by looking at floor maps of big art museums like the Metropolitan Art Museum, or the Louvre. My focus was to have a lot of wall surface area so that more paintings could be placed, while having large rectangle rooms to have many masterpeices in one room. I also had to make long and narrow corridors to trap player when the alarm goes off.

The shadowing could be done better but I wanted to capture the 8-bit theme. So I made every floor tile constantly check the player's position to decide if it should be shadowed or not. This took me around ten hours of creating 4x4 pixel size game objects and placing them as the floor.

When the player presses space while close to a painting; an 8-bit image of the original painting pops up, and a scrolling text gives information about the painting. This feels just like looking at a painting and reading the prompt next to it in an art museum. Even though all the artworks are in 8-bit it is very easy to distinguish the subject because of the wide veriety of colors.
